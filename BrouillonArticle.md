# Digital Sovereignty and Disaster Resilience: PULSE-AI, an Artificial Intelligence-enabled Framework for Assessing National ICT Resilience

## Abstract

The increasing frequency of natural disasters, cyber incidents, and large-scale infrastructure failures highlights the growing dependency of societies on resilient digital ecosystems. Disaster resilience can no longer be considered solely through physical infrastructures, as disruptions affecting telecommunications networks, cloud services, critical information systems, and emergency communications may significantly hinder national response capabilities. While several international indicators assess digital development, cybersecurity maturity, and disaster preparedness independently, few approaches provide an integrated assessment of national digital resilience.

This paper introduces PULSE-AI (Preparedness, Ubiquity, Localization, Sustainability, and Emergency Intelligence), an Artificial Intelligence-enabled framework designed to assess National ICT Resilience in the context of disaster management. The framework combines telecommunications resilience, digital sovereignty indicators, emergency communications capabilities, data governance, AI-enabled predictive analytics, and inclusion metrics into a unified National Digital Resilience Score (NDRS). PULSE-AI leverages Artificial Intelligence and Internet of Things technologies to dynamically assess critical infrastructures and support decision-makers before, during, and after disasters.

The proposed framework is particularly relevant for developing countries where resilient telecommunications infrastructures are fundamental to disaster preparedness and emergency response. Beyond technological considerations, PULSE-AI promotes an inclusive and sovereign approach to digital resilience by emphasizing governance, accessibility, and community-centered emergency communications.

**Keywords:** Disaster Management, Digital Sovereignty, Telecommunications Resilience, Artificial Intelligence, Internet Governance, Emergency Communications, ICT4D, Digital Inclusion.

## 1. Introduction

Natural disasters, climate-related crises, pandemics, cyber-attacks, and critical infrastructure failures increasingly demonstrate that modern societies depend upon resilient digital ecosystems. Telecommunications networks have become essential infrastructures supporting healthcare services, emergency communications, financial transactions, public administration, and disaster response mechanisms.

The concept of disaster resilience has traditionally focused on physical infrastructures such as roads, hospitals, electricity networks, and transportation systems. However, recent global crises have demonstrated that digital infrastructures constitute equally critical components of national resilience strategies.

The failure of Internet connectivity during emergencies may significantly affect:

- Emergency communications;
- Early warning systems;
- Access to critical public services;
- Healthcare systems interoperability;
- Crisis coordination mechanisms;
- Population information dissemination;
- Humanitarian response operations.

Consequently, disaster resilience should no longer be understood solely as the capacity to restore physical infrastructures but also as the ability to maintain critical digital services during crises.

This paper argues that digital sovereignty and national ICT resilience constitute fundamental pillars of disaster preparedness and proposes PULSE-AI as a multidimensional framework for assessing national digital resilience capabilities.

## 2. Related Works

Several international initiatives have contributed to measuring disaster preparedness and digital maturity.

These include:

- National Cybersecurity Index (NCSI);
- Global Cybersecurity Index;
- e-Government Development Index;
- Network Readiness Index;
- ICT Development Index;
- INFORM Risk Index;
- Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction.

Although these initiatives provide valuable assessments, they remain largely fragmented. Current approaches generally evaluate:

- Digital development;
- Cybersecurity capabilities;
- Disaster preparedness;
- Telecommunications infrastructures;
- Governance mechanisms.

Few frameworks simultaneously consider:

- Digital sovereignty;
- Telecommunications resilience;
- AI-enabled disaster management;
- Emergency communications;
- Data governance;
- Community inclusion;
- Critical services continuity.

PULSE-AI attempts to bridge these dimensions by introducing an integrated assessment model specifically designed for disaster resilience.

## 3. PULSE-AI Framework

The proposed framework is based upon six complementary dimensions.

### P : Preparedness

This dimension evaluates:

- Disaster management policies;
- National contingency plans;
- Emergency telecommunications frameworks;
- Institutional coordination mechanisms.

### U : Ubiquity

This component measures:

- Telecommunications coverage;
- Internet accessibility;
- Universal service availability;
- Accessibility requirements for vulnerable populations.

### L : Localization

Localization evaluates:

- Local hosting capabilities;
- Internet Exchange Points;
- National Data Centers;
- Community networks;
- Sovereign cloud infrastructures.

### S : Sustainability

Sustainability assesses:

- Infrastructure redundancy;
- Energy resilience;
- Critical services continuity;
- Recovery capabilities.

### E : Emergency Management

Emergency capabilities include:

- Early warning systems;
- Emergency communications;
- Interoperable public services;
- Crisis response mechanisms.

### AI : Artificial Intelligence Layer

The AI component enables:

- Predictive analytics;
- Infrastructure anomaly detection;
- Risk assessment;
- Disaster simulation;
- Dynamic resilience scoring.
